TheGlassPenguin’s Remixing Policy

Effective December 14, 2020

These are just some basic rules for remixing/backpacking my work on Scratch. You should follow these when you use the things I post. If you don’t agree with these, please don’t remix /backpack my stuff.

Let’s get into them. Don’t worry: I’ll keep it simple.

Remixing 

Remixing is a core part of Scratch, and I appreciate it. You can remix my projects, as long as your final cut falls under the “good remixes” category:

Good remixes:

  • Loops
  • Replacing my characters/sprites with yours (OC /sprite swaps)
  • Replacing my audio/voicelines with yours (audio swaps)
  • Adding things to my projects

Bad remixes:

  • Direct copies
  • Deleting all assets and just sharing a blank project
  • “FIRST REMIX!” projects

Recalls

Sometimes I may need to “recall” a project. If this happens, I will look through the remix tree  and contact you in the comments about an issue that may have been carried over into your remix, and what you need to do to stay in the clear.

Backpacking

You can backpack things that I use, as long as you give credit to the original creator. You might also want to include a link to the original content.  

Thanks for reading to the end! See you in the next project!
